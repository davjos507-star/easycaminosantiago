The English Way: the route of northern pilgrims

Long before the modern Camino boom, medieval pilgrims from England, Ireland, Flanders and Scandinavia had their own way to reach Santiago de Compostela. They boarded ships, crossed the Bay of Biscay and arrived in the Galician ports of Ferrol or A Coruña. From there they walked the remaining distance on what became known as the English Way — Camino Inglés.

Today the English Way from Ferrol covers 119 kilometres in 6 stages. It is one of the shortest official routes that still grants the Compostela, because it exceeds the 100km minimum required for the official pilgrim certificate. It winds through coastal estuaries, medieval towns and Galician countryside before entering the city of Santiago de Compostela.

The route is rated Easy: stages are short (14–25km), the terrain is mostly gentle and well signposted, and there are no high mountain passes. It is an excellent choice for first-time pilgrims, those with limited time, or anyone looking for a complete but approachable Camino experience.

English Way itinerary: 6 stages from Ferrol

Six manageable stages connect the port city of Ferrol with the Cathedral of Santiago. Below is a summary of each day.

Stage 1 Ferrol → Neda 14 km

A short opening stage that leaves the naval city of Ferrol and follows the Ría de Ferrol estuary towards Neda. A gentle warm-up with beautiful coastal views. You receive your pilgrim passport (credencial) at the start.

⬆ +180m⬇ −180m
Stage 2 Neda → Pontedeume 23 km

The longest stage of the route leads through forests and small villages to Pontedeume, a charming medieval town at the mouth of the Eume river. The historic centre and the 14th-century bridge make a memorable arrival.

⬆ +320m⬇ −320m
Stage 3 Pontedeume → Betanzos 14 km

A short and pleasant stage ending in Betanzos, one of the best-preserved medieval towns in Galicia. The Gothic churches of San Francisco and Santa María do Azougue are not to be missed.

⬆ +220m⬇ −220m
Stage 4 Betanzos → Bruma 25 km

The route leaves the coast behind and heads inland through eucalyptus and oak forests. This is the most demanding stage, but the green Galician landscape and quiet rural paths make it very rewarding.

⬆ +420m⬇ −350m
Stage 5 Bruma → Sigueiro 16 km

A comfortable stage through Galician villages and farmland, descending gradually towards Sigueiro on the banks of the Tambre river. The penultimate day before the great finale.

⬆ +180m⬇ −280m
Stage 6 Sigueiro → Santiago de Compostela 17 km

The final stage: the route joins the French Way at Monte do Gozo, the historic hill from which pilgrims have caught their first glimpse of the Cathedral towers for centuries. Entering the old city of Santiago and reaching the Plaza del Obradoiro is an emotion like no other.

⬆ +200m⬇ −250m

Frequently asked questions about the English Way

How long is the English Way?

The English Way from Ferrol to Santiago de Compostela is 119 kilometres, divided into 6 stages of between 14 and 25km each. It is one of the shortest Camino routes that still qualifies for the Compostela.

Do all km count for the Compostela on the English Way?

Yes. Starting from Ferrol you walk 119km — well above the 100km minimum required to receive the Compostela. Every stage counts. Note that starting from A Coruña (75km) does not meet the minimum requirement unless you walk the full distance from Ferrol.

When is the best time to walk the English Way?

The English Way can be walked all year round. Spring (April–June) and autumn (September–October) offer the most pleasant conditions: mild temperatures, green landscapes and fewer pilgrims. Summer is busy and can be warm. Winter is quiet but days are short and rain is more frequent in Galicia.

Is the English Way suitable for beginners?

Absolutely. The English Way is rated Easy and is one of the best Camino routes for first-time pilgrims. The stages are short (14–25km), the terrain is mostly flat or gently rolling, and there are no high mountain passes. Good walking shoes and a basic level of fitness are all you need.

How do I get to Ferrol to start the English Way?

Ferrol is well connected by train and bus from A Coruña (approximately 1 hour). A Coruña has an international airport with flights from many European cities, and is also served by high-speed AVE trains from Madrid. Our team can advise on the best travel connections for your specific dates.

What does the Easy Camino Santiago service include?

Our service includes: 6 nights accommodation with private bathroom, daily luggage transfer between stages, pilgrim passport (credencial), GPS route with detailed waypoints, and 24-hour WhatsApp support from our team throughout your journey.
