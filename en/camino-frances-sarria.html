How many days does the Camino from Sarria take?

The Camino de Santiago from Sarria is typically completed in 5 or 6 days, depending on each pilgrim's pace. The total distance is around 100 km, which allows you to obtain the Compostela on arrival in Santiago.

Itinerary — Stage by stage

5 memorable stages through the most authentic Galicia

1

Sarria → Portomarín

The baptism stage. You cross the Miño River on Portomarín's long bridge, leaving Sarria behind amid oak and chestnut forests. You pass the monastery of Barbadelo, one of the most beautiful on the Camino. You arrive at the charming village of Portomarín with its stone houses and Romanesque fortress church.

22.4 km+670m / -540m5-6hPortomarín
2

Portomarín → Palas de Rei

A gentle stage with continuous ups and downs through grey stone villages. You cross the Ligonde pass with splendid views over the Galician plateau. The hospitality of Galicia's inland villages will surprise you. You arrive at Palas de Rei, the hub of this stage with a great pilgrim atmosphere.

24.8 km+600m / -620m6-7hPalas de Rei
3

Palas de Rei → Arzúa

One of the most varied stages. You cross the Pambre River and its medieval castle, pass through Melide — famous for its octopus — and arrive at Arzúa, known for its Galician tetilla cheese. Here the pilgrim atmosphere intensifies as the Portuguese Way joins.

28.5 km+550m / -580m7-8hArzúa
4

Arzúa → O Pedrouzo

The penultimate stage. You walk through dense eucalyptus and pine forests creating an almost magical atmosphere. You pass through sleeping villages where roosters wake you at dawn. In O Pedrouzo the anticipation is at its peak: tomorrow you reach Santiago. The excitement is palpable in every conversation at the hostel.

19.3 km+380m / -360m5-6hO Pedrouzo
5

O Pedrouzo → Santiago de Compostela

The most special day. You cross Monte do Gozo — the "Mount of Joy" — where you see the Cathedral towers for the first time. You enter Santiago through the UNESCO World Heritage historic centre. The Plaza del Obradoiro awaits with the embrace of the Apostle and the Pilgrim Mass. A moment you will never forget.

19.4 km+250m / -280m5-6h🏆 Santiago de Compostela

FAQ — Camino from Sarria

Everything you need to know before walking the Camino

The distance from Sarria to Santiago is just over 100 kilometres, which meets the minimum required to receive the official Compostela. That is why it is one of the most popular options for those who want the full Camino experience in just a few days and arrive at the Cathedral with a completed passport.
Most people complete the Camino from Sarria in 5 or 6 stages, although the itinerary can be adjusted to suit your pace, fitness level, or the type of trip you want. Some prefer to walk further each day to reduce nights, while others add an extra stage to take it more slowly.
The Camino from Sarria is rated easy to moderate. It is a very suitable route for beginners, couples, groups of friends and many families, provided you have a basic level of fitness for walking several days in a row. It is also one of the best-served sections in terms of infrastructure, services and signage.
Yes. Starting from Sarria you can earn the Compostela as long as you complete the route on foot and stamp your pilgrim passport correctly along the way. Covering more than the 100-kilometre minimum, this route meets the official requirement set by the Cathedral of Santiago.
An organised Camino from Sarria typically includes pre-booked accommodation, daily luggage transfer between stages, trip documentation and support before and during the route. Depending on the package, it may also include breakfasts, insurance, additional transfers or extra nights in Santiago.
The Camino from Sarria is the ideal option for those seeking an authentic Camino de Santiago experience in less time. It qualifies for the Compostela, has accessible stages, a lively pilgrim atmosphere and an excellent combination of nature, Galician villages and historic heritage. That is why it is one of the most recommended routes for first-time pilgrims.
